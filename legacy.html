Their Lasting Legacy

The legacy that Bob and Sue Bomyea leave behind is one of love, faith, and unwavering dedication to the people and causes they cared about. Their 60-year love story, which began in 1964 at St. Anne Catholic Church, is a testament to the power of commitment, partnership, and choosing each other every single day. Through 22 years of military moves, new cities, and constant change, they built a foundation of love that never wavered. They showed their family and friends what it means to truly be partners in life - supporting each other's passions, working together in service, and finding joy in both the big adventures and the quiet moments. The quilts Sue made with her own hands, the meals Bob lovingly prepared, the friendships they nurtured through the Knights of Columbus, and the community they served through God Works meals - all of these are threads in the beautiful tapestry of their life together.

Bob and Sue's greatest legacy, however, isn't found in any single achievement or activity - it's found in the way they lived their values every single day. They taught us that service to others is one of the highest callings, that faith is meant to be lived out loud through action, and that love grows stronger when it's shared generously with the world. Their passion for life - whether on the golf course, at a campsite, or in the church kitchen - reminds us to embrace each day with enthusiasm and gratitude. The laughter they shared, the hands they held, the meals they served, and the love they gave so freely will continue to inspire everyone who knew them. Bob and Sue lived a life that truly mattered, and their example of love and service will continue to guide and inspire their family, friends, and community for generations to come.

"The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others." - Mahatma Gandhi

This quote perfectly embodies how Bob and Sue lived their lives.
